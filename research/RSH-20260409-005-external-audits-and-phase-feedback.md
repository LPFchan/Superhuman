## Question

What reusable lessons from prior audits and agent feedback should remain discoverable after the repo moves its canonical operating surfaces to the root?

## Key Findings

- Earlier audits consistently pushed toward stronger boundary discipline, better observability, clearer memory/state seams, and less hand-wavy “agent magic.”
- The phase feedback sequence reinforced a pattern: each wave needed more explicit deliverables, clearer exit criteria, and less reliance on implied architecture.
- These materials are still useful as critique and calibration inputs, but they are not current truth surfaces.

## Routing Outcome

- The durable guidance from these audits is now reflected across `SPEC.md`, `PLANS.md`, `REPO.md`, and the decision set.
- The archive family stays readable in place, but this memo is the reusable root-side index for why those files still matter.

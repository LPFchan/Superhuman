## Question

What product framing and interaction model best express Superhuman’s longer-horizon value beyond being an OpenClaw-shaped assistant fork?

## Key Findings

- The strongest product noun is `project workspace`, not `chat`, `thread`, or `work item`.
- Superhuman’s differentiator is not “another coding client.” It is a coherent home for memory, direction, research, and execution.
- Messenger surfaces make the most sense as capture, notification, approval, and quick-intake channels rather than canonical authoring surfaces.
- Mobile should mature as a project cockpit before it tries to mimic a full desktop IDE.
- Worklogs, decisions, research, truth, and plans need separate homes if the repo is going to function as durable project memory.
